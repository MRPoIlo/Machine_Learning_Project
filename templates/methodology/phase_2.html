Cleaning rules

The IRCA rural dataset published on datos.gov.co spans multiple years and comes with a mix of casing and trailing whitespace on categorical labels. Wilder applied the following rules to produce the working dataset.

  • Filter to the 2021–2024 window — recent and complete enough to model.
  • Trim whitespace on Nivel de riesgo rural labels so categories collapse correctly.
  • Coerce IRCArural to a numeric type, dropping non-parseable rows.
  • Cast Año to integer (source uses 2,024 formatting).

Variable engineering

The dataset already ships with two views of the same signal:

  • IRCArural — continuous index, 0 (safe) to 100 (sanitarily unviable).
  • Nivel de riesgo rural — five-class label derived from the index.

For modeling we'll keep both. The five-class label is the natural classification target; the numeric index supports regression baselines and ablation checks.

Cleaned dataset

Same view as Phase 1 — here the focus is verifying that the cleaning rules above produced a tidy table. Sort by IRCA to spot the most critical municipalities; filter to Sanitaryly not feasible to see the worst cases.

Deliverables
