Predicting Drinking Water Risk in Rural Colombia

A Machine Learning study that combines the official IRCA rural index with open climate and water infrastructure data to model drinking water risk across Colombian municipalities — and support evidence-based public health decisions.

01 — Overview

A data-driven look at rural drinking water in Colombia

Many rural communities still face uneven access to safe drinking water. By combining the official IRCA rural index with climate and infrastructure data, we can map risk patterns and highlight where action is needed most.

01

Problem

Across 2021–2024, roughly half of the rural municipalities sampled report some level of water-quality risk. Aggregating these signals by hand is slow and hides the patterns underneath.

02

Approach

We follow CRISP-ML to move from framing the public-health question through data engineering, modeling, evaluation and a working deployment on this Flask web app.

03

Outcome

A reproducible pipeline and a public-facing interface where anyone can explore the data, the cleaning steps and — once trained — the model's predictions.
