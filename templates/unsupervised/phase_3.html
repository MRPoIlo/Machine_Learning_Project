Dataset

The precipitation dataset comes from the Colombian open data portal (datos.gov.co) and contains rainfall measurements from weather stations spread across the country. Each record captures a single observation from a physical station along with its geographic coordinates, timestamp, and rainfall amount in millimetres.

Preprocessing

The raw CSV uses an unusual double-quote wrapping format and stores numeric values with Spanish locale decimal separators (commas instead of dots). The preprocessing script applied the following steps before any modelling could begin.

  • Strip outer double-quotes from every row and unescape inner quotes.
  • Convert ValorObservado, Latitud and Longitud from comma-decimal to dot-decimal format.
  • Parse FechaObservacion timestamps into proper datetime objects.
  • Extract hour, month and day_of_week as additional time features.
  • Normalise text columns (strip whitespace, uppercase) for consistent grouping.
  • Aggregate per station to produce a 33-row feature matrix — one row per physical station.

K-Means Clustering

K-Means partitions the 33 weather stations into K groups by minimising the distance between each station and its cluster centroid. We ran an elbow analysis evaluating inertia and silhouette score for K from 2 to 10. The silhouette score peaked at K = 2, confirming that two groups best describe the natural structure in this dataset.

  • Features used: mean precipitation, standard deviation, maximum precipitation, latitude, longitude, mean measurement hour.
  • Scaling: StandardScaler applied before training so no single feature dominates by magnitude.
  • Final K: 2, chosen by silhouette score (0.40).
  • Random state: 42, for reproducibility.

Results

With K = 2 the algorithm separated the 33 stations into two clearly distinct groups based on their precipitation behaviour and geographic position.
